.✦ ݁˖ My College Journey So Far

23rd of September, 2006

"..Yep, that's me. You're probably wondering how I ended up in this situation."

The photo above is one of the photo I took as part of the administration requirements for a scholarship I was accepted into. If you were to tell 1st semester Sasha that she'd be in her 4th semester, maxing out her credits every single semester, part of that organization she's always wanted to join, AND she's part of the scholarship program provided and organized by one of the biggest banks in Indonesia, she'd laugh in your face before accusing you of clinical insanity.

When I first arrived in Manado, the city I was born in yet I was utterly unacquainted with, I felt like I fit in until I realized that I did not. My first semester was full of self-discovery, trying to figure out who I am as a person because when you interact with so many new people in such short amount of time, you can start overthinking your responses and what impression you leave on people... right? Right?

From the very beginning, the engineering department has absolutely drilled into our heads that as peers, we should stick by each other. Solidarity and all that, though I'd personally say many cannot even give less.. care. Or maybe I just wasn't someone who would receive said care, which is why I say that. Huh, more to think about. Anywho, onto the more substantial part: I joined a religious organization framed to be our "home", Badan Tadzkir Fakultas Teknik, BTFT for short. Can I be honest? To this day, though the organization is not perfect, I do think that BTFT is one of the better ones. I've witnessed alumnis and students gather money to help a new student in need to pay off their tuition, but let's not get ahead of ourselves.

I would most definitely tell you that my second semester was my hardest. I had fallouts with an organization, I was on the center of a very big conflict that eventually was resolved, and I admittedly handled things quite badly. Even so, I do recognize that 2nd semester me was absolutely terrified, so that explains, not justifies, the way she handled things. My 2nd semester was also filled with me trying to get accepted for a scholarship. Spoiler alert: I get rejected for every. single. one. I remember I would stare at people in printing shops, printing out their employee statements or whatever for their jobs, or writing something, and thinking to myself: "When will it be my turn?"

Third semester me would have an answer for that. At the end of the semester, right after I was rejected from a scholarship I was so confident about, I halfheartedly pulled myself from the comfort of sulking and feeling sorry for myself on my bed and signed up for the Bakti BCA scholarship. Lo and behold, one night, after finishing my laboratory work and going to Burger King to immediately work on another assignment, I got the announcement that I was accepted. I felt like I was living a dream. It had to be a dream, I thought at the time. I don't have much to say for this third semester other than that. Despite happening in the end of the semester, it clearly defined my 3rd semester experience.

Fourth semester. My parents very kindly supplied me with a new laptop, I am on a scholarship, and I am more confident in myself. I also just got a haircut! I have very little to say since nothing's really happened (yet), but maybe I'll update this page when the semester ends. :)
