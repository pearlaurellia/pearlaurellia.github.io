.✦ ݁˖ Animating In Blender

Every video was animated by myself!

When I first decided to learn how to 3D model, my main motivation was to show off to my parents. Seriously. I wanted to show my mom and dad, and say "Look what I can do!"

My first project was a cup. A wonky tea cup. Sadly, I no longer have access to the original render because I have switched laptops since then. I then moved on to fancy and ambitious teacups, the Blender learner rite of passage donut, all the way up to some failed, cursed-looking character that I'm actually grateful I lost the files to. It would induce nightmares for you, probably, if you were to see it. At the time, I didn't have the right device to properly start animating. It was quite painful. See, in order to make an animation, Blender would have to render every single frame one. by. one. The pudding animation above has around 96 frames. One frame takes around 3-5 minutes or so, and that's already using only 5 iterations/samples for each frame. How many seconds did I get out of 96 frames? 4 seconds. My previous computer, bless it and its components, did not stand a chance.
